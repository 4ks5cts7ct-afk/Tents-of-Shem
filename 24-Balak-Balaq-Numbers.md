### Balak | Balaq | Numbers
Chapter 24
(Num 22:1–25:9): Micah 5:6–6:8



Surah Al-Humazah (Chapter 104)- "Woe to every scorner and mocker, who collects wealth and continuously counts it. He thinks that his wealth will make him immortal..."<br>
- The text targets human ego and material obsession. It warns that when a person becomes intoxicated by wealth, they develop a false sense of invincibility, which ultimately destroys them spiritually.<br>

Surah Al-Qalam 68:10-14- "And do not obey every worthless swearer... a slanderer, moving about with gossip, a preventer of good, transgressing and sinful, cruel, and moreover, an illegitimate pretender. Just because he possesses wealth and children..."<br>
-This describes a person who has been given natural gifts, influence, or understanding, but becomes "corrupted by his assets." The chapter warns that when God gives someone blessings or insights, and they use them for exploitation, God will "brand them on the muzzle" (an ancient Arabic idiom for ultimate public humiliation). <br>

Surah Al-A'raf 7:175-176: "And recite to them, [O Muhammad], the story of him to whom We gave Our signs, but he detached himself from them; so Satan pursued him, and he became of the deviants. And if We had willed, We could have elevated him thereby, but he adhered [instead] to the earth and followed his own desire. So his example is like that of the dog: if you chase him, he pants, or if you leave him, he pants. That is the example of the people who denied Our signs. So relate the stories that perhaps they will give thought."<br>

#### Numbers 24:1-25<br>
When Balaam saw that it pleased the Lord to bless Israel, he did not go, as at other times, to look for omens, but set his face toward the wilderness. 2 And Balaam lifted up his eyes and saw Israel camping tribe by tribe. And the Spirit of God came upon him, 3 and he took up his discourse and said,

> “The oracle of Balaam the son of Beor,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;the oracle of the man whose eye is opened,  
> the oracle of him who hears the words of God,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;who sees the vision of the Almighty,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;falling down with his eyes uncovered:  
> How lovely are your tents, O Jacob,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;your encampments, O Israel!  
> Like palm groves that stretch afar,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;like gardens beside a river,  
> like aloes that the Lord has planted,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;like cedar trees beside the waters.  
> Water shall flow from his buckets,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and his seed shall be in many waters;  
> his king shall be higher than Agag,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and his kingdom shall be exalted.  
> God brings him out of Egypt  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and is for them like the horns of the wild ox;  
> he shall eat up the nations, his adversaries,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and shall break their bones in pieces  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and pierce them through with his arrows.  
> He crouched, he lay down like a lion  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and like a lioness; who will rouse him up?  
> Blessed are those who bless you,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and cursed are those who curse you.”  

10 And Balak's anger was kindled against Balaam, and he struck his hands together. And Balak said to Balaam, “I called you to curse my enemies, and behold, you have blessed them these three times. 11 Therefore now flee to your own place. I said, ‘I will certainly honor you,’ but the Lord has held you back from honor.” 12 And Balaam said to Balak, “Did I not tell your messengers whom you sent to me, 13 ‘If Balak should give me his house full of silver and gold, I would not be able to go beyond the word of the Lord, to do either good or bad of my own will. What the Lord speaks, that will I speak’? 14 And now, behold, I am going to my people. Come, I will let you know what this people will do to your people in the latter days.”<br>

15 And he took up his discourse and said,

> “The oracle of Balaam the son of Beor,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;the oracle of the man whose eye is opened,  
> the oracle of him who hears the words of God,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and knows the knowledge of the Most High,  
> who sees the vision of the Almighty,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;falling down with his eyes uncovered:  
> I see him, but not now;  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I behold him, but not near:  
> a star shall come out of Jacob,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and a scepter shall rise out of Israel;  
> it shall crush the forehead of Moab  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and break down all the sons of Sheth.  
> Edom shall be dispossessed;  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Seir also, his enemies, shall be dispossessed.  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Israel is doing valiantly.  
> And one from Jacob shall exercise dominion  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and destroy the survivors of cities!”  

20 Then he looked on Amalek and took up his discourse and said,

> “Amalek was the first among the nations,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;but its end is utter destruction.”  

21 And he looked on the Kenite, and took up his discourse and said,

> “Enduring is your dwelling place,  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and your nest is set in the rock.  
> Nevertheless, Kain shall be burned  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;when Asshur takes you away captive.”  

23 And he took up his discourse and said,

> “Alas, who shall live when God does this?  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But ships shall come from Kittim  
> and shall afflict Asshur and Eber;  
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and he too shall come to utter destruction.”  

25 Then Balaam rose and went back to his place. And Balak also went his way.
