### Haftorah Judges 11:1–33

1. The Ammonites: The Line of Lot<br>
The Ammonites are not connected to Keturah or Ishmael. Their code traces back to the catastrophic collapse of Sodom. 
The Source Line: Abraham’s nephew was Lot. Following the destruction of Sodom, Lot’s younger daughter conceived a son with him named Ben-Ammi. <br>
The Tribal Identity: Ben-Ammi is the direct progenitor of the Ammonites.<br>
The Macro Link: Because Lot was Abraham’s nephew, the Ammonites are cousin-tribes to all Israelites. However, they are completely separate from the Egyptian (Hagar/Keturah) and Midianite (Keturah/Hagar) inheritance lines. <br>

2. The Gileadites: The Line of Isaac<br>
The Gileadites are pure, direct descendants of Isaac and Jacob. <br>
The Source Line: Abraham → Isaac → Jacob → Joseph → Manasseh. <br>
The Regional Name: Manasseh’s son was Machir, and Machir’s son was named Gilead. Gilead’s descendants settled the rugged, high-altitude Transjordan plateaus, becoming the clan known as the Gileadites. <br>
Jephthah’s Identity: Jephthah belongs strictly to the line of Isaac. He is an Israelite from the half-tribe of Manasseh. His father is literally named Gilead (or he is a son of the Gileadite clan patriarch). <br>

God Explicitly Validates Their Legitimacy<br>
If the purpose of recording the incest was to strip Moab and Ammon of their national legitimacy or territorial claims, the subsequent text would reflect that. But it does the exact opposite.<br>
In Deuteronomy 2:9 and Deuteronomy 2:19, God explicitly protects their real estate:<br>
"Do not harass the Moabites or provoke them to war, for I will not give you any of their land as a possession. I have given Ar to the descendants of Lot as a possession." <br>
"When you come near the Ammonites, do not harass them or provoke them to war, for I will not give you possession of any land belonging to the Ammonites. I have given it to the descendants of Lot as a possession."<br>
The text legally secures their borders because they are related to Abraham through Lot. The incestuous birth did not strip them of their earthly inheritance or their right to exist as sovereign nations. <br>

The cutoff in Deuteronomy 23:3–4 ("No Ammonite or Moabite shall enter the assembly of the Lord") is not triggered by their birth. The text explicitly lists the trigger: it was their active choice to hire Balaam to destroy Israel, and their refusal to offer bread and water. Their incestuous origins explain their internal spiritual wiring—the fact that they were born out of a traumatic, chaotic environment outside the covenantal boundary lines. The Torah records this to show that while they are legitimate nations with legitimate borders given to them by God, they lack the specific internal spiritual calibration required to stand inside the operations of the Sanctuary.<br>
