Just as in Parsha Naso, there is only one Surah used for this Haftorah: Surrah 2:246: “Have you not considered the leaders of the Children of Israel after Moses? But Allah is aware of the wrongdoers.”

When Surrah 2:246 was used in Parashat Naso, it allowed us to see how every minor administrative detail in Numbers is a direct, preventative answer to the exact political collapse that occurs in the Surah- the CHANGE from a leader/chief Nasi to a Malik. Because this verse contains the entire debate over wealth, lineage, military draft, and divine sovereignty, it acts as a massive mirror for the entirety of Parashat Naso. By keeping Surah 2:246 constant, it is demonstrating that the Torah didn't write these chapters as random pieces of legal trivia. The Sotah protocol, the Nazirite vow, and the Priestly Blessing are a unified, interlocking firewall. The Torah is showing Israel: “If you maintain your camp purity, if you allow ordinary citizens to wear the spiritual crown of a Nazir, and if you let the Priestly Blessing guard your homes, you will never experience the panic of Surah 2:246.
