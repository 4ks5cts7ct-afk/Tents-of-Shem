In modern structural analyses—such as academic papers published in journals like the Harvard Theological Review—scholars note that the Sabbath breaker's actions are deeply tied to the psychological trauma of the 40-year wilderness sentence handed down in Chapter 14. <br>

He is essentially saying: "If we are stuck in this desert to die anyway, we might as well go back to the economic rules and continuous labor of Egypt."  <br>

And also, all the inhabitants of the land melt away because of us.”  This conclusion provides the ultimate antidote to the multi-generational trauma of the wilderness.
